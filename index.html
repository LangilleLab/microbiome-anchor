Tentative Session Topics

  • Human Health (disease and AMR)
  • Computational approaches (new tools or assessment of tools)
  • Integrating Omic Technologies (non-genomic approaches,
    and/or combining metabolomics/transcriptomics, etc.)
  • Environmental Interactions (ocean, soil, plastics, etc.)
  • Host-associations
  • Viral and Eukaryotic communities
  • Putting Microbes to work
  • Ecological Modelling

Venue and Travel

  • Come take in the ocean air, sweeping coastal views, and experience the mysteries of Oak Island!
  • Microbiome ANCHOR is being held at Oak Island Resort & Conference Centre, - 36 Treasure Dr, Western Shore, Nova Scotia, Canada (Map) .
  • Oak Island Resort & Conference Centre is approximately 100km (~1 hr) from Halifax Stanfield International Airport Travel.
  • You may rent a car or take a taxi to Oak Island Resort. For taxi service, we recommend On Time Flight Taxi Services. They accommodate up to 6 passengers, offer student discounts, and can be booked online in advance.

Social and Recreation

  • After an inspiring day of talks, head to Three Sheets Bar + Social in the evening to continue networking and take in some live entertainment.
  • Interested in the mysteries of Oak Island? Boat tours are available here.
  • At the on-site marina you can access boat tours or rent a bike, paddle board, or kayak.
  • Oak Island Resort has multiple options for on-site recreation including an indoor pool, fitness room, tennis court, mini putt, and walking trials. Check out a full list of activities here
  • Just a short drive away are several great attractions.

Registration

Your registration fee includes:

  • 3 nights accommodation at Oak Island Resort (June 7-9)
  • Access to all conference proceedings:
    • Keynote presentation and all conference sessions (June 8-10)
    • Flash talks and poster sessions (June 8-9)
  • Meals include breakfast (June 8-10), lunch (June 8-10), dinner (June 8-9) and snacks (June 8-10).
  • Evening networking at Three Sheets Bar & Social (June 7, 8, and 9)
  • Use of Oak Island recreation facilities
